A Note to Our Families
on the 2026 AP Exam Results

Something wonderful happened this year.

Our students showed up for the College Board Advanced Placement exams, and the whole building leaned in with them. We want you to see what your children built together.

?
What is an AP score? AP exams are scored 1 to 5. A score of 3 or higher is considered qualifying and can earn students college credit at many universities. It signals that a student has done college-level work while still in high school.
2026 Newark Collegiate
53%
of our AP students earned a qualifying score of 3 or higher on at least one exam. Five years ago, that number was just 7 percent.
Reasons to celebrate
Perfect passing in two subjects
Every single student who sat for AP Seminar and AP African American Studies earned a qualifying score. One hundred percent.
Nine perfect fives
Nine Collegiate students earned the top score of 5 across Seminar, English, Calculus, Physics, and Precalculus.
A steady climb, year over year
Our schoolwide AP pass rate has grown every single year since 2022. From 7% to 53%.
Sophomores testing at senior level
Our tenth graders posted the same mean AP score as our twelfth graders. Your younger students are already ready.
From the School Leader's desk

To the families of Newark Collegiate,

These numbers are not the whole story. The story is your child, waking up early on a May morning to take a college-level exam. The story is the teacher who stayed late to review one more practice question. The story is you, believing your child could do it before anyone else in the room agreed.

We are proud, and we are not finished. Next year is Newark Collegiate's twentieth year, and we intend to make it the strongest yet. Thank you for trusting us with your children. We keep climbing.

With gratitude,
Sharmaine Lewis
School Leader, Newark Collegiate
