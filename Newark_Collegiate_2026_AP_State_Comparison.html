A note on the numbers: The three figures above use slightly different denominators. Ours is percentage of AP test-takers. The state and national are percentage of all public high school graduates: the College Board's official ranking metric, which asks what share of every graduating senior earned at least one qualifying AP score at some point during their four years. Most seniors nationwide never sit for an AP exam at all, which is why the state and national numbers are lower than a pass rate. The apples-to-apples comparison lives in the next section.

The trajectory nobody else has
Newark Collegiate 5-year growth
Our schoolwide AP pass rate went from 7% in 2022 to 53% in 2026. That's +46 percentage points in five years. Over that same span, New Jersey's statewide graduate 3+ rate moved a few points, and the national number moved about four. The state is a steady tide. We are a wave.
